Safri Duo   [ Music ]   [ Web Site ]

Morten Friis and Uffe Savery studied at the Royal Danish Conservatory of Classical Music. While both classically trained, their studies turned to percussive instruments. Since 1994, they've played a wide range of percussive instruments with a focus on marimbas and vibes. As a result, the "duo" produced several classical albums such as "Goldrush" and "Bach To The Future" up until 1998.

The following year, Universal Denmark's classical department discovered the two musicians and teamed them with trance producer Michael Parsberg. Having signed with a danish record label, the duo began work

to produce an album merging dance, trance and pop with ethnic percussive sounds.

"Played Alive", the team's best known single work, landed a Danish Grammy Award for "Best Club Hit 2000" while topping the European club charts. This and other singles gained support from worldwide artists the likes of DJ Tandu and Airscape.

Their latest album, "Episode II" was released on May 28, 2001. It features their trademark percussive sound blended with smooth electronica, transporting listeners to new aural worlds. -- OnTheWayDJ
