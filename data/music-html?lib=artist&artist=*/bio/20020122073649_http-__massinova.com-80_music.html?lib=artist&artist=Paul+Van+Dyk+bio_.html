Hailed as the "Messiah of Trance", Paul Van Dyk is among the forefathers of the trance revolution. His is an understanding of the movements of human imagination, emotion and energy. "I don't want to lead people", he says, "I want to tempt them, to create a new world for them, just for that very small moment when they are losing themselves in my music. I want to inspire them." Simply put, Paul Van Dyk creates music that folds around the soul of the listener and captures their emotions.

Born December 16th, 1971 in Eisenhuettenstadt, East Germany, Paul grew up in communist East Berlin. Prior

to Germanys reunification, he at least had an early window to the capitalist world via the cross-border airwaves. His early influences came from listening to the radio show of Monika Dietl. "I was really captivated by the music of 'The Smiths' and 'New Order'. It (the music) was very strong and emotional." Soon after, Paul began his experiments with a basic turntable setup. Moving to Berlin in the late 80's, he began to line up gigs around the city.
