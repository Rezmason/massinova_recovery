released his first track "Perfect Day" in 1992. The following year, he released the club hit of the year with a remix of "Love Stimulation" by Humate. He later released his first album, "45 RPM" and began regularly spinning sets at the legendary "E-Werk" club. He continued gaining respect through his remixes of many renowned artists including New Order, a source of his original inspiration. FInally, the release of "Seven Ways", his second album, firmly established Paul Van Dyk as one of the world's great electronic artists. The release of "Seven Ways" propelled Van Dyk and singles

including "Beautiful Place", "Forbidden Fruit" and "Words" to the top of British and European dance charts. Continuing to spin until Berlin's "E-Werk" club closed, he worked along side fellow superstar DJ's such as Nick Warren, BT, Dave Seaman and Sasha.

Not to be sheltered by success in the studio, Paul's talent is also recognized as a world renowned DJ. "As I began my career as a DJ I discovered that there is a really deep communication between DJ and people. I learned how to move the crowd and how to create atmosphere", he remembers.
