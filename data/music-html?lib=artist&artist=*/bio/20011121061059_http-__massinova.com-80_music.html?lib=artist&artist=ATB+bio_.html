ATB (Andre Tanneberger)   [ Music ]   [ Web Site ]

DJ Andre Tanneberger was born in Germany in 1973. Inspired to bring his own sounds to the trance community, he scored his first European club hit with 1999's release of "9 PM (Till I Come)". It rocketed him into the European spotlight and gained him international recognition as the hot single was played in clubs the world over. Later that year brought the release of his full-length album "Movin' Melodies".

For his second full-length release, Tanneberger collaborated with Heather Nova, Enigma and the Canadian rock band, Wild Strawberries. The double-disc release in 2000 was filled with

dance floor tunes and down tempo melodies. In mid-2000, ATB released "Killer", a remake of a Seak and Adamski track. It featured Drew Williams on vocals. That fall ATB had his third album release of "Two Worlds". --teknosys
