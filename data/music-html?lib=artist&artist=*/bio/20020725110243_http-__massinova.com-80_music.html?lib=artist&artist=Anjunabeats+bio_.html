Anjunabeats Records was established in the summer of 1999 by Jono Grant and Paavo SiljamŠki. The "Anjuna Boys" met at the university having already begun to make a name for themselves independently as record producers. Two heads seemed better than one, so they decided to team up and establish a label for releasing their uplifting and progressive trance music.

Opening up with Anjunabeats "Volume 1", the bomb had been dropped. The label made off to a good start with support from DJs such as Pete Tong, Paul Oakenfold and Paul Van Dyk. A club hit in its own right, Anjunabeats "Volume 1" went on

to secure the boys remix work for high profile acts.

Teaming up with fellow producer Tony McGuinness to form Above & Beyond, following their remix work on his own record, Nitromethane "Time To Die", they clicked and went on to remix Chakra "Home" for WEA. This was the lead mix and hit the number 1 spot in the UK club chart, causing big reactions on the nation's dancefloors. As a result, the remix requests rolled in.
