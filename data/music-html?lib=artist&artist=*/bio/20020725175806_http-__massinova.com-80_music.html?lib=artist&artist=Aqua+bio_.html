It took years of slow development and a series of steps for Aqua to make known it's fun and furious dance beats. It began back in 1989 with Claus Norreen and Soren Rasted working together to compose music for a Danish movie soundtrack. Rene McLean, at the time, was working as a DJ in Norway when he soon discovered Lene Grawford Nystrom singing on a TV show. In a meeting in Denmark, Rene introduced Lene to Soren and Claus. Shortly after that they formed a band call 'Joyspeed'. In their short time as 'Joyspeed', the group release the single "Itzy Bitzy" which showed lackluster success in a

one week appearance on Swedish charts before vanishing.

In an attempt to reinvent itself the group dissolved their old contract and secured a new one with the Danish label Universal Music. In September of 1996, they released their debut single "Roses are Red" as Aqua. It stayed on the charts for several weeks and eventually made platinum. It also received a Grammy nomination for the Best Danish Dance Publication. The follow-up single in February of 1997 "My Oh My" also hit a number one spot on the charts.
